About Kurdistan

Kurdistan means "Land of the Kurds." In Iraq’s laws, it’s called the Kurdistan Region, and the government there is called the
Kurdistan Regional Government (KRG). The KRG is sometimes called "Kurdistana Bashur" or "Southern Kurdistan" because
it’s located in the southern part of the broader Kurdistan area.

Iraqi Kurdistan is an autonomous region within Iraq. It is surrounded by four countries: Iran to the east, Turkey to the north,
Syria to the west, and the rest of Iraq to the south. The capital city of the region is Erbil, also known as Hawler. The Kurdistan
Regional Government helps take care of the region and its people.

According to the Fondation-Institut Kurde de Paris (2017), the Kurdish population is estimated to be between 36 and 46 million
people. It is the largest ethnic minority in the world without its own state (CNN 2024).

Landscape

Kurdistan, Iraq (Photo by Peter Hansen on Unsplash)

When you visit Kurdistan for the first time, you’ll see that the most noticeable feature is the mountains. There’s a saying among Kurds:
"No friends but the mountains." This means that, for many years, the mountains have been the only safe place for Kurds, away from
rulers or countries that try to control them.

The mountains are very important to the Kurdish people. They have helped shape the history, traditions, and culture of the Kurds.
The mountains have also been used by Kurdish fighters, called Peshmerga, to hide and protect themselves while fighting against
unfair rulers.

Kurdistan is made up of many different mountain ranges, like the Zagros Mountains in the center and north, the Taurus and Pontus
mountains in the east, and the Amanus mountains in the north. There are also Kurdish areas in the Alburz Mountains in Iran and the
Pontian mountains in Turkey.

Kurdish Language

The official languages of the Kurdistan Region for government use are Kurdish and Arabic. Kurdish belongs to the Indo-European
language family. The different dialects of Kurdish language include the following:

  1. Sorani
  2. Kurmanji
  3. Hawrami or Gorani
  4. Zaza
The two main dialects of Kurdish are Sorani and Kurmanji, which are spoken by the majority of people. Other less commonly spoken
dialects include Hawrami (also called Gorani) and Zaza.
